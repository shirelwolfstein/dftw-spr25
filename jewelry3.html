Jewelry

  • Stud
    • Characterized by a straight bar that allows for the healing of a straight piercing channel. Mostly flat on one side and with a decoration on the other, but can have designs on either side or other ends (balls, disks, butterfly backs, etc.) 
    • If the area allows for it (space-wise), most of these piercings are recommended to be healed with the stud and then can be changed to a hoop.
      • Forward helix
      • Conch
      • Helix
      • Mid
      • Lobe 
      • Tragus
      • Flat
      • Nostril
      • Medusa
      • Snakebites
      • Labret 
      • Monroe
      • Ashley
  • Curved Barbell
    • Post of the jewelry is slightly curved into a banana-like shape, with either end having a bigger stopper with balls, spikes, discs, etc.
      • Snug
      • Anti tragus
      • Rook
      • Eyebrow
      • Angel fangs
      • Snake eyes
      • Vertical labret
      • Navel 
  • Straight Barbell
    • Post of the jewelry is straight, with either end having a bigger stopper with balls, spikes, discs, etc. The posts tend to be longer than studs and the ends create the distinction.
      • Industrial
      • Bridge
      • Tongue piercing
      • Nipples
  • Hoop/Horseshoe
    • The hoop is a continuous piece of jewelry shaped into a circle, and the horseshoe is a barbell curved into a circular shape with a gap in the middle for either end of the jewelry to attach.
      • Daith
        • Although this can be healed with a curved barbell, people should be aware that it is best to heal with a ring since curved bars run the risk of flipping and making the angle of the piercing canal heal so that nothing sits flush. Less visible often times too.
      • Septum
      • Smiley
  • Surface staple bars
    • The bar sits under the first layer of skin and the two posts come up to sit above the surface. This jewelry limits rejection.
      • Frog eyes
      • Anti eyebrow
  • Anchor
    • An anchor is placed so that a flared base sits below the skin, and the post comes out of the skin so that interchangeable tops can sit flush to the skin. This piercing jewelry cannot be taken in and out, just removed fully. The head of the jewelry can be switched out for alternative looks.
      • Back dermals

Hoop/horseshoe

Although most piercings do not benefit from healing with hoops, septums and daiths tend to be the exceptions. These ensure the channel stays flush and jewelry can be worn with correct angles in the future.

  • smth
  • other

Staple

Bars sit under the skin with two visible posts sticking out. These do not need to be surgially removed although they may be difficult to get out of the skin. Corners must be curved 90 degrees and not rounded to limit likelihood of rejection.

  • smth
  • other

Anchor

Cannot be taken out of the body, the compatible heads can be changed out. Removal is painful but once it is out it is fully removed. (Difficult to heal flush.)
