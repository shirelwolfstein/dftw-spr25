Anatonmy

  • Ear
    1. Forward helix
      • Anatomy dependent, must be space behind for the earring to sit flush to the skin and ridge must be wide enough to hold an earring.
    2. Daith
      • Anatomy dependent, must be space behind for the earring to sit flush to the skin and ridge must be wide enough to hold an earring.
    3. Industrial
      • Anatomy dependent, must be space behind for the earring to sit flush to the skin and ridge must be wide enough to hold an earring. The part of the upper ear that would be behind the bar also must be flat enough as to not disturb or push on the bar. This provides room for cleaning, making sure the piercing does not migrate, and ensuring that the jewelry does not cut into the flat of the ear. 
    4. Conch
      • Rare cases may not allow for the piercing, as it needs a flat surface, but it is very uncommon to not have the anatomy for a conch piercing. Some people also may have scar tissue from ears being pinned back that could cause extended healing time, but this would generally not be considered anatomy dependent.
    5. Snug
      • Anatomy dependent, must have the cartilage sticking out prominently enough to pierce through with space on either side for the jewelry to sit flush. 
    6. Anti tragus
      • Slightly anatomy dependent, must have a big enough ridge to hold an earring.
    7. Helix
      • Rare cases may not allow for the piercing, as it needs a semi-flat or open space, but it is very uncommon to not have the anatomy for a helix piercing. Some ears naturally do not have space and fold tightly in on themselves but this would generally not be considered anatomy dependent.
    8. Mid
      • Slightly anatomy dependent, must have enough space where the ridge of the ear is flat to properly hold an earring.
    9. Lobe
      • Not anatomy dependent
    10. Tragus
      • Somewhat anatomy dependent, ridge must be wide enough to hold an earring.
    11. Rook
      • Somewhat anatomy dependent, the ridge must be big enough to hold an earring and there must be enough space to accommodate for jewelry without interference.
    12. Flat
      • Not considered anatomy dependent

  • Face  

    1. Frog eyes
      • Not considered anatomy dependent
    2. Tongue vertical
      • Not typically considered anatomy dependent, even those with ‘tongue ties’ who cannot stick their tongues out very far have been able to get this piercing with experienced professionals.
    3. Snake eyes
      • Not considered anatomy dependent
    4. Eyebrow
      • Considered to be somewhat anatomy dependent, must have enough room and thick enough skin to hold the jewelry.
    5. Anti eyebrow
      • Not typically anatomy dependent, must have enough room and thick enough skin to hold the jewelry.
    6. Smiley
      • Not typically considered anatomy dependent but must have enough skin to hold jewelry. 
    7. Angel fangs
      • Not typically considered anatomy dependent piercings, as long as you have a small ridge above the top lip, but people with small lips are able to have this piercing as well.
    8. Labret
      • Not typically considered anatomy dependent.
    9. Monroe
      • Not considered anatomy dependent
    10. Ashley
      • Not considered anatomy dependent.
    11. Snakebites
      • Not considered anatomy dependent.
    12. Vertical labret
      • Anatomy dependent, must be enough of a ridge from the jut of the bottom lip for the jewelry to sit right and hold.
    13. Medusa
      • Anatomy dependent, the frenulum connecting the upper lip and gums can potentially get in the way of the piercing.
    14. Septum
      • Not typically considered anatomy dependent but must have enough space on either side of the septum for the jewelry to fit, so severely deviated septums may not be compatible. Some people also do not have a big ‘sweet-spot’, in this case it is possible to pierce through the cartilage, although the healing process will be longer.
    15. Bridge
      • Anatomy dependent, must be enough skin and space between the bone and the skin to allow for jewelry to sit properly and not migrate, and to hold jewelry.
    16. Nostrils
      • Not considered anatomy dependent

    17. Body 

      1. Navel
        • Anatomy dependent, must be enough of a lip or ridge for the jewelry to cling onto and not migrate. Belly button also must be an ‘innie’ for the jewelry to sit correctly.
      2. Nipples
        • Anatomy dependent, must have enough space for jewelry to hold onto, inverted does not necessarily mean that someone does not have compatible anatomy, but they need to be able to come out enough. 
      3. Back dermals / anchor dermals
        • Not anatomy dependent
      4. Surface piercings
        • Anatomy dependent, must be in an optimal area, mostly flat and does not move constantly. Must have enough room and thick enough skin to hold the jewelry.
