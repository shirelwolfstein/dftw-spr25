Pricing

      Ear

    • Forward helix
      • Time- precise and slower piercing so that the needle doesn’t puncture the cartilage behind the forward helix. Relative medium due to mapping and angle, but no symmetry.
      • Difficult area to reach, but due to the straight angle of piercing it isn’t terribly difficult. Delicate process requiring specific cut receiving tube.
      • Symmetry- none, typically done on one ear
    • Daith
      • Time- precise and slightly slower piercing due to crowded area that may result in lower visibility. Relative medium because it usually does not require mapping for additional piercings and usually no symmetry, but the angle is often tricky.
      • Difficulty- the angle of the piercing is hard to get to between folds, and the piercing requires a curved needle for proper placement and piercing transfer.
      • symmetry- none, typically done on one ear
    • Industrial
      • Time- marking may take longer due to the angle being slightly variable depending on the ear and client preference, two puncture holes take a bit longer. These are not difficult to reach, so it is relatively quick.
      • Difficulty- requires proper angling and using two needles can at times be awkward, but it is in an easily accessible part of the ear.
      • Symmetry- none, typically done on one ear
    • Conch
      • Time- lacks complex mapping in most cases, no symmetry. Quick.
      • Difficulty- Relatively open area, fairly easy. Jewelry insert can sometimes be difficult but not notably so.
      • Symmetry- none, typically done on one ear
    • Snug
      • Time- figuring out if anatomy allows for the piercing often takes the most time, making sure that there is proper space and would not be creating two separate piercings. Relative medium. 
      • Not very difficult to perform, puncture angle is straightforward.
      • Symmetry- none, typically done on one ear
    • Anti tragus
      • Time- lacks complex mapping in most cases, no symmetry. Quick.
      • Not difficult to perform, puncture angle is straightforward.
      • Symmetry- none, typically done on one ear
    • Helix
      • Time- lacks complex mapping in most cases, but different set ups, angles, and multiples require more planning. No symmetry. Quick.
      • Not a difficult area to reach, puncture angle is straightforward. Easy to move the ear as well. 
      • Symmetry- none, typically done on one ear
    • Mid
      • Time- lacks complex mapping, no symmetry. Quick.
      • Not a difficult area to reach, puncture angle is straightforward. Easy to move the ear as well. 
      • Symmetry- none, typically done on one ear
    • Lobe
      • Time- symmetry makes piercing process much longer, checking multiple times for symmetry. Relative medium to longer.
      • Not a difficult area to reach, puncture angle is straightforward. Easy to move the ear as well.
      • Symmetry- done on both ears the majority of the time
    • Tragus
      • Time- difficult area to reach to put earrings in sometimes, relative medium.
      • Not a difficult area to reach, but due to the straight angle of piercing, getting the needle through is sometimes uncomfortable and more delicate.
      • Symmetry- none, typically done on one ear
    • Rook
      • Time- precise and slightly slower piercing due to crowded area that may result in lower visibility. Relative medium because it usually does not require mapping for additional piercings and usually no symmetry, but the angle is often tricky.
      • Difficulty- the angle of the piercing is hard to get to between folds, and the piercing requires a curved needle for proper placement and piercing transfer.
      • Symmetry- none, typically done on one ear
    • Flat
      • Time- lacks complex mapping in most cases, but different set ups, angles, and multiples require more planning. No symmetry. Quick.
      • Not a difficult area to reach, puncture angle is straightforward. Easy to move the ear as well.
      • Symmetry- none, typically done on one ear

    • Face 

    • Frog eyes
      • Time- sanitization may take longer due to it being in the mouth, relative medium.
      • Not a difficult area to reach, requires even mapping, but it is not a complex piercing and visible surface. 
      • Symmetry- none
      • This piercing is popular but not advisable, considered unsafe. The venom piercing allows for much better movement of the tongue and healing, the frog eyes staple bar jewelry is best choice for the piercing but still ties the different tongue muscles together and doesn't allow for the natural elastic movement of the tongue. 
    • Tongue vertical
      • Time- sanitization may take longer due to it being in the mouth, relative medium.
      • Not a difficult area to reach, requires even mapping, but it is not a complex piercing and visible surface.
      • Symmetry- none if done traditionally in the center of the tongue, but can be safely done on either side of the tongue as well, at times multiple pairs depending on the length of the tongue. Double tongue piercing side to side refers to venom piercing. 
    • Snake eyes
      • Time- sanitization may take longer due to it being in the mouth, relative medium.
      • Not a difficult area to reach, requires even mapping, but it is not a complex piercing and visible surface.
      • Symmetry- none, done on the tip
      • This piercing is popular but not advisable, considered unsafe. This ties the different tongue muscles together and doesn't allow for the natural elastic movement of the tongue. The placement also allows the metal of the jewelry to constantly hit the back of the client’s teeth and gums, causing nerve damage, gum recession, enamel damage, etc. May cause a lisp. Not only not advisable but piercers who still offer the piercing at all are generally considered dangerous and not credible due to how dangerous it is.
    • Eyebrow
      • Time- Due to the different placements and more specifically varying angles of the piercing, mapping often takes a bit. Relative medium to long.
      • Not a difficult area to reach, and due to the straight angle of piercing it isn’t terribly difficult.
      • Symmetry- none, typically done on one side but increasing popularity to do it on two sides.
    • Anti eyebrow
      • Time- Due to the different placements and more specifically varying angles of the piercing, mapping often takes a bit. Relative medium to long.
      • Not a difficult area to reach, and due to the straight angle of piercing it isn’t terribly difficult.
      • Symmetry- none
    • Smiley
      • Time- relative medium to quick
      • Difficult area to reach, but due to the straight angle of piercing it isn’t terribly difficult.
      • Symmetry- none
    • Angel fangs
      • Time- mapping to ensure symmetry takes a bit of time, and ensuring the safety of a client’s mouth may slow the process. Relative long.
      • Difficult area to reach, especially with the proximity to the nose. Due to the straight angle of piercing it isn’t terribly difficult. 
      • Symmetry- mapped out on face and angled to clients preference, height and angle are both factors of symmetry but some may be adjusted to fit the facial features of the client and lip shape. Must be marked first and then done quickly in succession to ensure that swelling does not affect symmetry.
    • Labret
      • Time- mapping for symmetry often takes time with specific looks, after finding center of the bottom lip, relative medium to quick.
      • Not a difficult area to reach, must be performed carefully due to proximity to the mouth and gums, but due to the straight angle of piercing and mobility of the lips, they are not terribly difficult to perform.
      • Symmetry- none if done traditional, depending on configuration or set up
    • Monroe
      • Time- relative medium to quick.
      • Slightly difficult area to reach, must be performed carefully due to proximity to the mouth and gums, but due to the straight angle of piercing and mobility of the lips, they are not terribly difficult to perform.
      • Symmetry- none, typically done on one side
    • Ashley
      • Time- relative medium to quick after placement in the center.
      • Not a difficult area to reach, must be performed carefully due to proximity to the mouth and gums, but due to the straight angle of piercing and mobility of the lips, they are not terribly difficult to perform.
      • Symmetry- none
    • Snakebites
      • Time- mapping to ensure symmetry takes a bit of time, and ensuring the safety of a client’s mouth may slow the process. Relative long.
      • Not a difficult area to reach, must be performed carefully due to proximity to the mouth and gums, but due to the straight angle of piercing and mobility of the lips, they are not terribly difficult to perform.
      • Symmetry- this set up of piercing entails the paired set that mirror each other on either side below the bottom lip. Must be marked first and then done quickly in succession to ensure that swelling does not affect symmetry.
    • Vertical labret
      • Time- ensuring that the piercing is straight takes a bit of time, the piercing itself is relatively quick.
      • Not difficult to pierce if the depth if correct
      • Symmetry- none, typically done in the middle of the lip. Can serve as an alternative to snakebites in order to reduce risk of gum recession, but this can only be performed if the client’s lip anatomy allows. 
    • Medusa
      • Time- choosing a good spot and ensuring it does not interfere with mouth movement or rub too much against the teeth. This may take more time, relative long.
      • Difficult area to reach, piercing into the mouth so close to the gums to is a delicate process with cramped space. Piercing it from the inside of the lip towards the outside, but this requires experience and precision.
      • Symmetry- none
    • Septum
      • Time- finding the ‘sweet-spot’ is not very time consuming, ensuring the piercing is straight both vertically and is important, but relatively quick.
      • Difficult area to reach, but due to the straight angle of piercing it isn’t terribly difficult.
      • Symmetry- none
    • Bridge
      • Time- requires mapping to ensure that the piercing is straight and symmetrical in the middle of the face. Relative medium – long.
      • Not a difficult area to reach, due to the straight angle of piercing it isn’t terribly difficult, but proper depth must be known.
      • Symmetry- none, can be mapped and paired
    • Nostrils
      • Time- not always mapped out, quick.
      • Not a difficult area to reach, puncture angle is straightforward. Jewelry insertion is also not particularly difficult.
      • Symmetry- none, typically done on one side but increasing popularity to do it on two sides.

    • Body

    • Navel
      • Time- must look for optical centering, quick.
      • Not a difficult area to reach, due to the straight angle of piercing it isn’t terribly difficult.
      • Symmetry- none, typically done in the center of the belly button
    • Nipples
      • Time- mapping and preparation take a while to ensure they be pierced with little damage and client angle preference.
      • Not a difficult area to reach, due to the straight angle of piercing it isn’t terribly difficult. Piercer must be aware of what is safe or not.
      • Symmetry- typically done on both sides
    • Back dermals / anchor dermals
      • Time- relative medium, paired dermals require more time with mapping.
      • Depending on the area, not difficult to reach, must make sure not to push the needle too far and insert an even level for the jewelry to sit properly.
      • Symmetry- none, but often done in pairs
    • Surface piercings
      • Time- Due to the different placements of the piercing, mapping often takes a bit. Relative medium to long.
      • Depends on the area that is being pierced, but not terribly difficult.
      • Symmetry- none, can be paired
