Healing

All piercings heal at their own pace depending on the client’s body and aftercare procedures. The healing process has stages, and the lack of irritation or pain after a new piercing does not indicate that it is fully healed, this will always take around or more than the estimated time given when pierced. Matured piercings come after the piercing has been taken care of after an extended time of having the piercing. 

It is important to clean piercings, rubbing alcohol and hydrogen peroxide are often used beforehand to clean the area but are often too harsh on the open wound of a new piercing. Instead, it is advisable to purchase a sterile saline solution to apply to either side of the piercing channel and try not to touch the piercing or area surrounding it to avoid irritation and trapping germs. A solution can be made by bringing water to a boil and dissolving non-iodized salt in the water. Apply with a q-tip after cooling or use a shot glass to cover the area and submerge it in water. 

Materials of jewelry affect healing. Surgical and stainless steel can be used, but are not the best. Implant grade titanium and gold are the best for the body. Titanium can be anodized for different colors as well.

The right guage size also matters, it is important that a piercing is not always wire thin while healing a piercing so the body heals around it instead of trying to expel it, like a splinter. This can be a conversation between the piercer and client to find the best solution on a case to case basis.

Chains can be healed THROUGH. So can string. Threaders should be worn only for relatively short periods of time, no matter how long the area has been healed for.
