Piercings

Needles are NOT reusable, and a hollow bladed needle is the best for minimizing trauma to the body. Piercing guns force through the skin and cartilage; and they can shatter the area resulting in various healing processes, though they are often extended and grueling if not causing additional health issues that must be addressed by a medical professional.

    Ear

    • Forward helix

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same
      • Pierced through the outer fold of the ear, closest to the face on the ear itself.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1, (they are nicely paired or grouped if you have space but they are considered different piercings).

    • Daith

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same
      • Pierced through the fold of the ear above the tragus and inner ear canal, and leading to the concha.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1
      • Although this can be healed with a curved barbell, people should be aware that it is best to heal with a ring since curved bars run the risk of flipping and making the angle of the piercing canal heal so that nothing sits flush. Less visible often times too.

    • Industrial

      • Industrial piercings, also known as scaffold piercings in the UK/Ireland
      • Pierced through the outer fold, or helix, of the ear. It generally goes horizontal on an angle from the outer helix to the top of the ear or closer to the face.
      • Number of puncture holes- 2
      • Can be healed with a bar or with separate straight barbells. The bar ensures that the angles of the piercings stay aligned, but the constant movement through canals can cause irritation and prolonged healing time.

    • Conch

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same
      • Pierced through the inner part of the outer ear, often in the middle closest to the outer fold.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1

    • Snug

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same
      • Pierced through the fold of the concha cartilage before it meets the mid helix.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1

    • Anti tragus

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same
      • Pierced through the ridge of cartilage above the lobe of the ear, near the lower part of the inner fold of the ear, leading to the concha.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1

    • Helix

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same
      • Pierced through the outer fold of the ear, along the curve. Often done towards the outer or farthermost edge, but can be done anywhere and at various angles for different looks.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1, often multiples (they are nicely paired or grouped if you have space but they are considered different piercings).

    • Mid

      • Mid or mid-helix
      • Pierced through the outer fold of the ear, along the curve towards the middle of the ear height-wise.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1, often multiples (they are nicely paired or grouped if you have space but they are considered different piercings).

    • Lobe

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same
      • Pierced through the flat area of skin at the bottom of the ear.
      • Number of puncture holes- technically 1 per each ear , but typically done in pairs so considered 2

    • Tragus

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same
      • Pierced through the triangle shaped cartilage blocking the inner ear canal, connected to the face.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1

    • Rook

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same
      • Pierced through the ridge of cartilage between the helix and the daith, towards the face.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1

    • Flat

      • Flat, flat helix
      • Pierced through the upper cartilage of the ear, directly through to the back of the ear.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1

    Face

    • Frog eyes

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same, known by the same thing, often mistaken with the venom piercing, which are two traditional vertical tongue piercings placed on either side of the tongue. Venom piercing is the safe alternative to frog eyes piercing.
      • Pierced through the top of the tongue, spanning across the surface.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1
      • This piercing is popular but not advisable, considered unsafe. The venom piercing allows for much better movement of the tongue and healing, the frog eyes staple bar jewelry is best choice for the piercing but still ties the different tongue muscles together and doesn't allow for the natural elastic movement of the tongue. 

    • Tongue vertical

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same, traditional vertical tongue piercing
      • Pierced through the outer fold of the ear, closest to the face on the ear itself.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1
      • Can be safely done on either side of the tongue as well, at times multiple pairs depending on the length of the tongue. Double tongue piercing side to side refers to venom piercing. 

    • Snake eyes

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same, tongue tip piercing
      • Pierced horizontally through the tip of the tongue
      • Number of puncture holes- 1
      • This piercing is popular but not advisable, considered unsafe. This ties the different tongue muscles together and doesn't allow for the natural elastic movement of the tongue. The placement also allows the metal of the jewelry to constantly hit the back of the client’s teeth and gums, causing nerve damage, gum recession, enamel damage, etc. May cause a lisp. Not only not advisable but piercers who still offer the piercing at all are generally considered dangerous and not credible due to how dangerous it is.

    • Eyebrow

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same, surface piercing
      • Pierced through skin around the eyebrow, traditionally vertically or at varying angles, but can be done above or below the eyebrows horizontally. Traditional style has one end of the jewelry on the bottom and one at the top, the jewelry being in the skin behind the eyebrow itself.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1 

    • Anti eyebrow

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same, surface piercing
      • Pierced through skin higher on the cheekbone, below the outer corner of the eye
      • Number of puncture holes- 1 

    • Smiley

      • Smiley piercing, upper lip frenulum piercing
      • Pierced through the thin skin connecting the upper lip and the gums.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1

    • Angel fangs

      • Angel fangs, symmetrical vertical labrets on upper lip
      • Pierced through the upper lip, right above the top lip to the middle of the top lip.
      • Number of puncture holes- 2

    • Labret

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same
      • Most commonly pierced below the bottom lip, but can be placed anywhere around the mouth. Different placements and numbers of labret piercings often have slang terms referring to specific configurations, like snakebites, spider bites, dolphin bites, dahlias, and more.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1

    • Monroe

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same
      • Pierced through the skin above the top lip, emulating Marylin Monroe's beauty mark, off to the side of the lips.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1

    • Ashley

      • Ashley piercing, inverse vertical labret
      • Pierced through the center of the bottom lip straight through.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1

    • Snakebites

      • Snakebites, paired labrets
      • Pierced through the skin below the bottom lip on either side.
      • Number of puncture hole- 2, but are considered separate piercings. These can be done with only 1, or can be done with more than 2.

    • Vertical labret

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same
      • Pierced through the center of the bottom lip to under the ridge below the bottom of the lip.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1

    • Medusa

      • Medusa piercing, philtrum piercing
      • Pierced above the center of the top lip, within the dip of the cupid’s bow.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1

    • Septum

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same
      • Pierced through the ‘sweet-spot’ of the septum or separation of the two nostrils within the nose. The sweet spot is towards the tip of the nose, under the cartilage and before the thick skin. 
      • Number of puncture holes- 1

    • Bridge

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same
      • Pierced through the skin of the top of the nose bridge, between and below the eyebrows.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1

    • Nostrils

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same
      • Pierced through the skin of the nostril all the way through one side of the nose.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1

    Body 

    • Navel

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same, belly button piercing
      • Pierced through the skin above the belly button, exiting within the belly button.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1

    • Nipples

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same
      • Pierced through the protruding part of the nipple.
      • Number of puncture holes- 2, they are considered separate piercings but are typically paired. It is possible to only get one.

    • Back dermals / anchor dermals

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same
      • Needle inserted under the first layers of skin on either side of the same entrance hole. The needle does not pierce all the way through, it is then removed and an anchor is placed below the skin. This piercing jewelry cannot be taken in and out, just removed fully. The head of the jewelry can be switched out for alternative looks. 
      • Number of puncture holes- 1

    • Surface piercings

      • Slang and professional/anatomical name are the same
      • Pierced through any patch of skin, must be a staple bar so that it sits under the first layer of skin and the two posts come up to sit above the surface. This jewelry limits rejection.
      • Number of puncture holes- 1
